# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We are committed to making this project a welcoming, respectful, and inclusive space for everyone.

We expect all contributors, participants, and community members to help create a positive environment where people can learn, share ideas, and collaborate safely and respectfully.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- Being respectful and considerate in communication
- Welcoming new contributors and helping them get started
- Offering constructive feedback
- Being open to different viewpoints and experiences
- Focusing on ideas and solutions rather than personal attacks
- Respecting academic integrity and intellectual ownership

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, discrimination, or personal attacks
- Offensive, insulting, or demeaning comments
- Sharing content in bad faith or with the intent to embarrass others
- Plagiarism or misrepresentation of others' work
- Any behavior that makes others feel unsafe or unwelcome

## Our Responsibility

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying standards of acceptable behavior and for taking appropriate action in response to any behavior that violates this Code of Conduct.

## Enforcement

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it to the project maintainer.

Reports will be handled respectfully and confidentially. All concerns will be reviewed fairly, and appropriate action may be taken depending on the situation.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including:

- GitHub issues and pull requests
- Discussions and comments
- Contributions to the codebase
- Any communication related to this project

## Acknowledgement

By participating in this project, you agree to treat others with respect and help maintain a constructive, friendly, and supportive community.

Thank you for being part of this project and helping make it a positive place to learn and collaborate.
